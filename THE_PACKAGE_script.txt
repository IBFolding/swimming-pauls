"THE PACKAGE"
A Short Film Written by 50 Pauls

FADE IN:

INT. COFFEE SHOP - MORNING

JAMES (30s, disheveled suit) stares at a MYSTERIOUS PACKAGE on the table. 
Unmarked. Brown paper. About shoebox size.

His PHONE BUZZES. He ignores it.

JAMES
(whispering to himself)
If I open it... everything changes.

Behind him, BARISTA SARAH (20s, tattooed) watches with curious eyes.

SARAH
You gonna stare at that all day?

JAMES
I'm deciding.

SARAH
Deciding what?

JAMES
Whether I want my life to be 
different.

Sarah studies him. Something in his voice.

SARAH
Sometimes different is better.

JAMES
Sometimes it's worse.

The PACKAGE VIBRATES. Once. Twice.

James freezes. Sarah backs away.

SARAH
What the hell is that?

JAMES
That's the question, isn't it?

EXT. COFFEE SHOP - CONTINUOUS

Through the window, we see James reach for the package.

His hand hovers.

JAMES (V.O.)
Three days ago, I was an accountant.
Three days ago, I had a routine.
Three days ago, I didn't know 
packages could vibrate.

His PHONE rings. Unknown number.

JAMES
(into phone)
Hello?

VOICE (O.S.)
(filtered, mechanical)
Don't open it.

JAMES
Who is this?

VOICE (O.S.)
Someone who opened theirs.

CLICK. Dial tone.

James stares at the phone. Then the package.

JAMES
(to himself)
Shit.

He RIPS the package open—

INT. JAMES'S APARTMENT - FLASHBACK - THREE DAYS EARLIER

James enters. Exhausted. Another late night at the office.

His APARTMENT is beige. Everything beige. Blinds closed.

He drops his briefcase. Collapses on the couch.

TV plays infomercials. Volume too low to hear.

James opens a drawer. Pulls out a bottle of PILLS.

Reads the label: "For the life you wanted."

He stares at the bottle. Doesn't take one.

Instead, he opens his laptop.

Searches: "jobs that don't require Excel"

Results: "Writer, artist, professional taste-tester..."

He laughs. A sad sound.

CLOSES laptop.

JAMES (V.O.)
I used to write stories.
Before the mortgage.
Before the fear.

His PHONE buzzes. Text from MOM:

"Dinner Sunday? Linda's son got promoted."

James sighs. Doesn't respond.

That's when he notices it.

On his doorstep.

The PACKAGE.

INT. COFFEE SHOP - PRESENT

James stares into the open box.

His face changes. Confusion. Then wonder. Then fear.

SARAH
Well? What is it?

James looks up at her. Eyes wet.

JAMES
It's...
(beat)
It's my manuscript.

SARAH
Your what?

JAMES
The novel I wrote. Ten years ago.
The one I burned.

He pulls out PAGES. Yellowed. Typed. His name on the cover.

JAMES (CONT'D)
I burned this. I watched it turn 
to ash. This is impossible.

Sarah moves closer. Reads a page.

SARAH
"The man who received impossible 
things..."
(looks up)
That's this. That's you.

JAMES
I wrote this before any of this.

The package VIBRATES again.

James digs deeper. Pulls out a SECOND manuscript.

Different title. Different year.

JAMES (CONT'D)
This one I never finished. Never 
wrote the ending.

He flips to the last page. There IS an ending now. 
In his handwriting.

SARAH
(reading over his shoulder)
"And so he chose. Not the life he 
had. Not the life he feared. But 
the life that terrified and 
thrilled him in equal measure."

JAMES
(whispering)
I didn't write that.

SARAH
It's your handwriting.

JAMES
It's NOT ME.

A CUSTOMER (40s, suit) approaches. Eyes on the package.

CUSTOMER
You opened it.

JAMES
Do I know you?

CUSTOMER
You will.
(beat)
What did you get?

James clutches the manuscripts tight.

JAMES
Who are you?

CUSTOMER
I'm the guy who got a key.
(beat)
To a door. To somewhere... else.
I opened it yesterday. Walked 
through. Came back. Can't stop 
thinking about it.

JAMES
What was there?

CUSTOMER
Everything I didn't choose.

The Customer's eyes are haunted. But alive.

CUSTOMER (CONT'D)
You got stories. I got a door. 
Woman across town got a plane 
ticket. Guy in Denver got a 
ring—proposed this morning.

SARAH
What is this? Some cult?

CUSTOMER
No cult. Just... possibilities.
Arriving. For people who need 
them.

JAMES
I don't need stories. I needed 
to forget them.

CUSTOMER
Did you?

James looks at his burned, now unburned, manuscript.

JAMES
No.

CUSTOMER
Then finish it.

The Customer walks out. Doesn't look back.

Sarah sits across from James. Her shift forgotten.

SARAH
What are you going to do?

JAMES
I don't know.

SARAH
Yes you do.

James looks at her. Really sees her for the first time.

The tattoos. The worn copy of "On Writing" peeking from her bag.

JAMES
You write?

SARAH
Poetry. Bad poetry. But... yeah.

JAMES
Can I read it?

SARAH
Can I read yours?

They stare at each other. The moment stretching.

JAMES
It's not good. It was never good.
That's why I burned it.

SARAH
Then why did it come back?

James has no answer.

EXT. COFFEE SHOP - LATER

James and Sarah walk. The manuscripts in a bag between them.

SARAH
Where are we going?

JAMES
My apartment. I need to show you 
something.

SARAH
This isn't a serial killer thing, 
right?

JAMES
(smiling for the first time)
Not yet. Check back in an hour.

Sarah laughs. A real laugh.

INT. JAMES'S APARTMENT - NIGHT

The beige apartment. But different now.

Laptop open. James typing furiously.

Sarah reading his manuscript. Marking pages.

SARAH
This scene here—where he quits his 
job—it's too fast. He needs to 
doubt more.

JAMES
(typing)
More doubt. Got it.

SARAH
And here, when he meets her... make 
it messy. Real first meetings are 
awkward. They don't flow like 
movie dialogue.

James stops typing. Looks at her.

JAMES
How would you write it?

SARAH
Me?

JAMES
You clearly know what you're 
talking about.

SARAH
I make coffee for a living.

JAMES
And you write poetry. And you read 
carefully. And you see things.

Sarah hesitates. Then:

SARAH
Okay. But you can't laugh.

JAMES
Promise.

She takes the laptop. Types:

SARAH
(typing, reading aloud)
"He didn't say anything clever. 
She didn't say anything clever. 
They just... existed near each 
other. And somehow, that was 
enough. For now."

JAMES
That's better than anything I 
wrote.

SARAH
It's honest.

They look at each other. The space between them charged.

JAMES
Sarah.

SARAH
Yeah?

JAMES
I don't want to go back. To before 
this package. To before you.

SARAH
I'm just a stranger who reads 
fast.

JAMES
You're the first person who read 
this and didn't tell me to get a 
real job.

SARAH
Fuck real jobs.

James laughs. Really laughs.

JAMES
Fuck real jobs.

EXT. CITY STREET - DAWN

James and Sarah walk. Empty streets. The world waking up.

SARAH
What happens at the end? In the 
new ending. The one that appeared.

JAMES
He finishes the book. He publishes 
it. It's... modestly successful.

SARAH
And?

JAMES
And he keeps writing. Bad days and 
good days. But he keeps going.

SARAH
That sounds nice.

JAMES
It sounds terrifying.

SARAH
Same thing.

They stop walking. Face each other.

JAMES
The manuscript says we... meet. 
That we work together. That we—

SARAH
Don't tell me. I don't want to 
know.

JAMES
Why?

SARAH
Because if I know, I might try to 
make it happen. Or avoid it. And 
either way, it's not real.

JAMES
What if it's already written?

SARAH
Then I'll be pleasantly surprised. 
Or devastated. But it will be 
mine.

James nods. Gets it.

JAMES
So what now?

SARAH
Now you write. And I read. And we 
see.

JAMES
That's terrifying too.

SARAH
(beat)
You gonna open any more packages?

JAMES
Not if I can help it.

SARAH
Good. Mystery solved. Life 
continues.

JAMES
Does it?

SARAH
Doesn't it always?

INT. COFFEE SHOP - MONTHS LATER

The same coffee shop. Different energy.

James sits at his usual table. LAPTOP open. Typing.

A STACK OF PAPERS beside him. A manuscript. Finished.

TITLE PAGE: "THE PACKAGE - A NOVEL BY JAMES CHEN"

Sarah brings him coffee. Their routine now.

SARAH
Query letters?

JAMES
Twenty so far. Nineteen rejections.

SARAH
One maybe?

JAMES
One maybe.

SARAH
That's all you need.

She sits across from him. Pulls out her own NOTEBOOK.

Poems. Filled with her handwriting. Her crossed-out lines.

JAMES
Reading tonight?

SARAH
Open mic. Terrified.

JAMES
You'll be great.

SARAH
You haven't heard them.

JAMES
Doesn't matter.

They smile at each other. Easy now. Familiar.

Through the window, we see a FIGURE across the street.

The CUSTOMER from before. Watching them.

He holds up his own PACKAGE. Unopened.

Then walks away. Disappears into the crowd.

James sees him. Says nothing.

Just turns back to his laptop.

Keeps typing.

JAMES (V.O.)
Some people get packages. Some 
don't. I don't know why me. I 
don't know why now. But I know 
this:

He looks at Sarah. She's writing now too.

JAMES (V.O.) (CONT'D)
The story isn't what arrives. 
It's what you do after.

He types the final words:

FADE OUT.

THE END
