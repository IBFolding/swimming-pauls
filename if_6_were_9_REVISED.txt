FADE IN:


EXT. SKYLAND STUDIO BACKLOT - BATTLEFIELD SET - PRE-DAWN

Silence. Then --

A RED FLARE shoots into the grey sky. Illuminates a WWI trench system stretching across fifty yards of studio backlot. Mud. Barbed wire. The works.

This cost 1.5 million dollars. Built overnight.

LIZZY CHEN (24), camera operator, grips her A-camera rig like it's a life raft. Sweat beads on her forehead despite the morning chill.

She's not ready.

Behind her, the CREW buzzes like a nervous hive. But Lizzy only sees the trench. The scope. The impossibility of it all.

D.P. VINCENT "VINNIE" CRANE (60s) appears beside her. White linen suit flowing in the breeze. Einstein hair. Wired on coffee and hubris.

VINNIE
(quietly, for her ears only)
You know what the difference is between
a good shot and a great shot?

Lizzy shakes her head.

VINNIE (CONT'D)
Fear.
(beat)
Good shooters capture action. Great ones
capture the moment someone realizes
they're going to die.

Lizzy swallows.

VINNIE (CONT'D)
Today's budget? 1.5 million. One day.
One take.

LIZZY
Vinnie, I don't know if I can --

VINNIE
(turning to the crew, booming)
Listen up! This is basically a oner. No
cutting. No hiding. Lizzy, my dear,
you're on the frontline.

The crew turns. All eyes on her. Lizzy wants to disappear.

VINNIE (CONT'D)
You leave that camera, you kill the take.
You fuck the focus, you kill the take.
You need to pee?

(beat, dead serious)

Pee your pants.

Lizzy forces a nod. Vinnie smiles -- warm and terrifying.

VINNIE (CONT'D)
Don't fuck up. No pressure.

He drifts away, already shouting at the GAFFER about light temperature.

LARRY (30s), Lizzy's first AC and boyfriend, materializes at her elbow. Grizzled. Calm. The eye of this hurricane.

LARRY
(quiet)
He's trying to psych you out.

LIZZY
It's working.

LARRY
You know why he picked you?

LIZZY
Because I work cheap?

LARRY
Because you're better than his usual guys.
He just can't admit he needs a 24-year-old
woman to save his shot.

Lizzy looks at him. Sees the worry he's hiding.

LIZZY
If I blow this...

LARRY
You won't.

LIZZY
But if I do --

LARRY
(touching her arm)
Then we both go down. Together. Romantic,
right?

She almost laughs. Almost.

LIZZY
I actually have to pee.

LARRY
Ten minutes to first team. Go. I'll prep
the rig.

She hands him the camera -- a moment of trust -- and sprints toward the porta-potties.

Larry watches her go. His smile fades.

MIKEY (20s), second AC, appears with lens cases.

MIKEY
She gonna be okay?

LARRY
(loading mags)
She has to be.

MIKEY
And if she's not?

Larry looks at the battlefield set. The trenches. The money burning by the minute.

LARRY
Then I take the fall. Not her.

Mikey absorbs this. Nods.

MIKEY
Extra primes are loaded. Cans of air.
We're good.

LARRY
Stay close today. I mean close.

MIKEY
Like we're dating?

LARRY
Like your job depends on it.

A DISTANT SHOUT -- the ASSISTANT DIRECTOR with a bullhorn.

A.D. (O.S.)
First positions! We go in five!

Larry checks his watch. Looks toward the porta-potties. No Lizzy.

LARRY
Shit.

He starts walking that way --

-- when Lizzy rounds the corner at a run. Hair wild. Eyes wilder.

LIZZY
I'm here. I'm here.

She reaches for the camera. Larry hesitates -- just a beat.

LARRY
You sure?

LIZZY
(looking past him, at Vinnie)
No.

(but she takes the camera)

But I'm doing it anyway.

They share a look. History in it. Fear. Love. The weight of everything.

VINNIE (O.S.)
(through megaphone)
Lizzy! My dear! Are we rolling?

Lizzy settles the camera on her shoulder. Checks the gate. Focus.

LIZZY
(into her headset)
Speed.

VINNIE (O.S.)
And... action.

Lizzy steps forward. Into the trench. Into the shot.

The camera rolls.

FADE OUT.


THE END
